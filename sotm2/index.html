Famous Botanical Garden

Indian Botanic Garden, Howrah:

The Indian Botanic Garden in Howrah, near Kolkata, is a vital center for botanical research and conservation. Spanning over 273 acres, it is home to more than 12,000 plant species, including a diverse collection of indigenous and exotic flora. One of its most famous attractions is the Great Banyan Tree, which is over 250 years old and covers more than 14,000 square meters. The garden offers serene walking paths, making it a perfect spot for nature lovers and researchers alike to explore and study the rich biodiversity of India.

Lalbagh Botanical Garden, Bangalore:

The Lalbagh Botanical Garden in Bangalore, a legacy of Hyder Ali and Tipu Sultan, is one of India's most beautiful gardens. Spanning 240 acres, it boasts rare plants from across the world, including Persia and France. The centerpiece is the Glass House, which hosts the vibrant flower shows that attract thousands of visitors every year. With its lush greenery, serene lakes, and historic significance, Lalbagh is a haven for nature enthusiasts.

Lloyd Botanic Garden, Darjeeling

Nestled in the Himalayan foothills, the Lloyd Botanic Garden in Darjeeling offers a unique collection of native Himalayan plants. Established in 1878, it is known for its rare orchids, vibrant rhododendrons, and a variety of ferns. The garden serves as an important conservation site for the region’s biodiversity, offering visitors a tranquil escape with breathtaking views of the mountains.

Government Botanical Garden, Ooty

The Government Botanical Garden in Ooty, established in 1848, is a stunning example of colonial-era garden design. Sprawling across 55 acres, it features an impressive collection of exotic plants, colorful flower beds, and a fossilized tree that dates back over 20 million years. The garden’s serene atmosphere and diverse plant species make it a top destination for tourists and botanists alike.
